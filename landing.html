The Konark Sun Temple was built in 1250 CE during the reign
of the Eastern Ganga King Narsimhadeva-1
from stone in the form of a giant ornamented chariot dedicated
to the Sun god, Surya. In Hindu Vedic iconography
Surya is represented as rising in the east and
traveling rapidly across the sky in a chariot drawn by seven horses.
